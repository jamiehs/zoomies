Flat-Out Racing

Click anywhere on the page and watch the cars race to your cursor. The cars use a realistic rear-axle bicycle model — they steer, accelerate, brake, and drift when stopping at speed.

The Grid

Three cars, three different lines into the first corner. In the margins, you'll notice them jostling for position — they avoid each other using a simple separation force so they never quite collide, but they always race.

Programmatic API

Open the browser console and try:
driver.cars[0].driveTo(200, 200) — moves only car 0
driver.driveTo(400, 300) — moves all cars
driver.addCar({ color: '#ff0' }) — adds a car

About the physics

The steering model places the pivot point at the rear axle: the front wheels turn up to 35°, and the car rotates according to ω = (v / L) · tan(δ), where v is speed, L the wheelbase, and δ the steering angle. Braking distance is computed from kinematics so the cars always stop exactly on target.
